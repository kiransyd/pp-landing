# Instagram Ad Creative Prompts for PracticePapers.io

## Instructions
Use these prompts with Google Gemini to generate Instagram ad creatives. Each prompt targets a different angle to test which messaging drives the most waitlist signups.

---

## Prompt 1: The "Fake Study" Call-Out (Pain-Focused)

```
Create an Instagram ad creative (1080x1080 static image) for a product called PracticePapers.io — an HSC Mathematics practice platform for Year 11 and 12 students in NSW, Australia.

CONCEPT: "Fake Study" call-out
The ad should feel like a reality check. It targets students who spend hours highlighting notes, rewatching YouTube videos, and colour-coding their study schedule — but still go blank in exams.

VISUAL DIRECTION:
- Clean, modern, editorial design (think Linear, Arc Browser, Raycast aesthetic)
- Warm off-white background (#F8F7F5) with dark text (#18181B)
- Terracotta/coral accent colour (#E8543A) for emphasis
- Use Inter or a similar clean sans-serif font
- Minimalist, no clutter — lots of white space

TEXT ON THE IMAGE:
- Top (small, muted): "HSC Mathematics · NSW · 2025"
- Main headline (large, bold): "You've studied for 3 hours. But can you actually solve Q14?"
- Subtext (smaller): "Highlighting isn't practice. Real exam-style papers with full worked solutions are."
- CTA button or banner at bottom: "Join 250+ students on the waitlist → practicpapers.io"

TONE: Direct, honest, slightly confrontational but empathetic. Like a smart older friend who got a 99 ATAR telling you the truth.

DO NOT include: stock photos of students, generic motivational quotes, bright neon colours, or cluttered layouts. This should feel premium and trustworthy.
```

## Prompt 2: The "Night Before" Confidence Hook (Aspiration-Focused)

```
Create an Instagram ad creative (1080x1080 static image) for PracticePapers.io — an HSC Mathematics practice platform for NSW Year 12 students.

CONCEPT: "Night Before the Exam" confidence
This ad should tap into the feeling every student wants: sitting down the night before an exam and feeling genuinely ready. Not anxious. Not cramming. Ready.

VISUAL DIRECTION:
- Dark, sophisticated background (#18181B or very dark zinc)
- Clean white text with a terracotta/coral accent (#E8543A)
- Minimal design — one strong headline, supporting text, CTA
- Premium feel, like a luxury product ad but for education
- Subtle mathematical notation or equations as a very faint watermark/texture in the background (optional)

TEXT ON THE IMAGE:
- Main headline (large, bold, white): "The night before your exam, you should feel ready."
- Supporting line (smaller, light grey): "Real HSC-style papers. Step-by-step worked solutions. Practice by topic. Track your weak areas."
- Bottom CTA: "Join the waitlist · practicpapers.io"
- Small social proof line: "250+ HSC students already signed up"

TONE: Calm, confident, aspirational. Not hype — quiet confidence. The kind of energy a student who's done the work radiates.

DO NOT include: alarm clocks, stressed students, red urgency colours, countdown timers, or "limited spots" scarcity tactics. This brand doesn't use dark patterns.
```

## Prompt 3: The Feature Showcase (Product-Focused, Carousel Frame 1)

```
Create an Instagram ad creative (1080x1080 static image) for PracticePapers.io — the first slide of a carousel ad showcasing the product.

CONCEPT: Product feature showcase — "This is what actual exam prep looks like"
Show what the platform does in a visual, scannable way. This is for students who are past the awareness stage and want to see what they're signing up for.

VISUAL DIRECTION:
- Clean white/off-white background (#F8F7F5)
- Show a stylised UI mockup (not a real screenshot — a designed representation) of the platform with:
  - A practice paper question card showing an integration problem
  - A "Reveal Solution" button
  - A progress tracker showing topic mastery percentages (e.g., Calculus 84%, Trigonometry 72%, Probability 48%)
- Use terracotta (#E8543A) for progress bars and accent elements
- Dark text (#18181B), clean sans-serif font

TEXT ON THE IMAGE:
- Top headline: "Stop guessing. Start knowing."
- Bottom: "HSC Maths practice papers with worked solutions · practicpapers.io"
- Small CTA: "Join the waitlist →"

TONE: Clean, product-forward, informative. Let the UI speak for itself.

DO NOT include: fake testimonials on this slide, pricing, or long paragraphs. Keep it visual and scannable.
```

## Prompt 4: The Social Proof / Testimonial (Trust-Focused)

```
Create an Instagram ad creative (1080x1080 static image) for PracticePapers.io featuring a student testimonial.

CONCEPT: Authentic social proof
Show a real-feeling student quote that validates the product. The tone should feel like a screenshot from a group chat or Discord message — not a polished corporate testimonial.

VISUAL DIRECTION:
- Off-white background (#F8F7F5) or very light warm grey
- The testimonial should look like a message bubble or card — slightly rounded corners, subtle shadow
- Student name and course level shown (e.g., "Anika S. · Extension 2")
- Small PracticePapers.io logo at the bottom
- Terracotta accent (#E8543A) for the quote marks or highlight

TESTIMONIAL TEXT (on the image):
"genuinely the most useful thing I've found for ext 2. the worked solutions actually show you HOW to get the marks, not just the answer."
— Anika S., Extension 2

BOTTOM CTA: "Join 250+ students on the waitlist → practicpapers.io"

TONE: Authentic, casual, lowercase energy. This generation can smell corporate copy instantly. The quote should feel real.

DO NOT include: star ratings, headshot photos, formal language, or "I rate this 5/5" style quotes. Keep it natural.
```

## Prompt 5: The Parent-Targeted Ad (Secondary Audience)

```
Create an Instagram ad creative (1080x1080 static image) for PracticePapers.io, targeting parents of HSC students.

CONCEPT: "Give them the right tools"
This ad speaks to parents who want to support their child's HSC preparation but don't know how. It positions PracticePapers.io as the practical, expert-backed tool their child actually needs.

VISUAL DIRECTION:
- Clean, warm, trustworthy design
- Off-white background (#F8F7F5) with dark text (#18181B)
- Slightly more traditional/premium feel than the student-facing ads
- No cartoons or childish elements — this is for adults making a purchasing decision

TEXT ON THE IMAGE:
- Top (small): "For parents of HSC Maths students"
- Main headline: "They don't need more study hours. They need better practice."
- Supporting text: "PracticePapers.io gives your child real HSC-style papers with step-by-step worked solutions — so they actually learn the method, not just the answer."
- Bottom CTA: "Join the waitlist · practicpapers.io"
- Trust line: "Built by HSC markers and high-achieving alumni"

TONE: Warm, reassuring, authoritative. Like advice from a trusted teacher at parent-teacher night.

DO NOT include: report cards, grades, stressed imagery, or "your child is falling behind" fear-based messaging. This brand is about empowerment, not anxiety.
```

## Prompt 6: The Reels/Story Hook (9:16 Video Script)

```
Write a script for a 15-second Instagram Reel ad for PracticePapers.io — an HSC Maths practice platform for NSW students.

CONCEPT: Quick hook + product reveal
This should stop the scroll in the first 1-2 seconds and deliver the value prop fast.

SCRIPT:

[HOOK — Text on screen, 0-2 seconds]
"POV: You've been studying for 4 hours but can't solve a single past paper question"

[TRANSITION — 2-5 seconds]
Quick cut to a clean UI showing PracticePapers.io
Text overlay: "There's a better way to study for HSC Maths"

[PRODUCT SHOTS — 5-12 seconds]
Show (as motion graphics or screen recordings):
1. Browsing practice papers by topic
2. Attempting a question, then revealing the worked solution
3. A progress dashboard showing weak areas improving

Text overlays during this section:
- "Real HSC-style papers"
- "Full worked solutions"
- "Track your weak spots"

[CTA — 12-15 seconds]
Text on screen: "Join 250+ students on the waitlist"
"practicpapers.io"

MUSIC: Lo-fi study beats or trending calm audio
PACING: Fast cuts, clean transitions, no talking head needed
TONE: Relatable, aspirational, clean

DO NOT include: a talking head, loud music, aggressive sales language, or "link in bio" (use the direct URL instead).
```

## Testing Strategy

For waitlist conversion testing, run these ads in the following priority:

| Priority | Ad | Angle | Target |
|----------|----|-------|--------|
| 1 | Prompt 2 | Aspiration (Night Before) | Students 16-18, NSW |
| 2 | Prompt 1 | Pain (Fake Study) | Students 16-18, NSW |
| 3 | Prompt 4 | Social Proof (Testimonial) | Students 16-18, NSW |
| 4 | Prompt 5 | Parent-targeted | Parents 35-55, NSW |
| 5 | Prompt 3 | Product showcase | Retargeting warm audience |
| 6 | Prompt 6 | Reels/Story | Broad reach, students |

**Recommended A/B test:** Run Prompts 1 and 2 simultaneously with $20-30/day each for 3-5 days to identify whether pain-focused or aspiration-focused messaging drives more waitlist signups. Then scale the winner.

**Audience targeting suggestions:**
- Location: New South Wales, Australia
- Age: 16-18 (student ads), 35-55 (parent ads)
- Interests: HSC, Mathematics, Year 12, ATAR, Study tips, Australian education
- Lookalike: Upload waitlist emails as a seed audience once you have 100+
